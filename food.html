I'm eight years into my adult life and food seems to be a recurring theme. However, I no longer want to spend all my time working in restaurants. Instead I want to use my skills and knowledge to meaningfully contribute to the health and happiness of others by impacting lives.

In 2015 I took a position teaching middle schoolers in public schools about food and how to cook it. Here is our blog.

Here are some books, writing and people who inspire me:

  • Marion Nestle and her book & blog about Food Politics
  • Food Justice by Robert Gottlieb & Anupama Joshi
  • Harold Mcgee and his writing about the science of food
  • Slow Food Movement founded by Carlo Petrini
  • Stephen Ritz for being and incredible educator
  • José Bové for radically caring about food and the environment
  • Michael Pollan For first getting me interested in the ethical realities of food
  • All the inspiring chefs who continue to innovate
